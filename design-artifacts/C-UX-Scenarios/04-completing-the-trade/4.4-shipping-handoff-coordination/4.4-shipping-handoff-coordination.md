## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Generate a platform-mediated shipping label so Tom never hands his home address directly to a stranger — this page is the direct answer to his top fear (13/15) and a Must-Have feature per the Feature Impact Analysis.

**User Situation:** Transaction terms settled, ready to actually ship the book.

**Success Criteria:** Tom gets a ready-to-use shipping label (or a drop-off point) without typing or revealing his home address to the other party at any point.

## Page Sections

### Section: Shipping Method
**Purpose:** Offer a choice between a mailed prepaid label and a drop-off locker/point, both of which avoid exposing Tom's address to the recipient directly.

### Section: Generate Label
**Purpose:** One-tap generation of a shipping label using the platform's own address-masking system — Tom's real address is used by the carrier integration but never shown to the counterpart.

### Section: Privacy Reassurance
**Purpose:** Explicit, visible confirmation ("Your address stays private") — directly counters the fear this page exists to solve, not just implicitly.

## Technical Notes

- Requires integration with a shipping carrier API supporting address-masked/anonymized labels (common pattern in P2P marketplaces — e.g. proxy addresses). This is a hard technical dependency for the whole "safe handoff" promise.
