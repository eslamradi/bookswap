# Maya the Bookworm — Priority 1

> Target Group 1 of 3 · [← Back to Trigger Map](../trigger-map.md)

---

## Who Maya Is

Maya reads constantly — a book a week, sometimes more. Her shelves have been full for years, but she can't stop buying or acquiring new books because the *next* read is always more exciting than admitting she has too many. She's the kind of reader who has opinions about translations and remembers which friend lent her which book five years ago.

## Psychological Profile

Maya's relationship to books is tied to **identity**, not just consumption. Being well-read is part of how she sees herself and how she wants to be seen. She gets genuine pleasure from the "treasure hunt" — finding a book she wouldn't have picked off a bestseller table, discovering an author through a stranger's shelf rather than an algorithm. This is different from simply wanting cheap books: she could afford to buy new, but **discovery and resourcefulness are the point**.

She's also quietly anxious about her own volume. Buying yet another book feels slightly indulgent; trading one away and getting something "new to her" back resolves that tension without asking her to actually read less or buy less.

## Internal State

When Maya thinks about her book pile, she feels **conflicted** — proud of how much she reads, mildly guilty about the shelf space, and genuinely excited by the idea of a source of "new" books that doesn't require more spending or more square footage. When she imagines a bad swap experience (wrong condition, a ghosted partner), she feels **burned and wary**, protective of a hobby that's supposed to be a source of joy, not a chore.

## Usage Context

**1. Access/Discovery:** Hears about bookswap from a friend or a bookish online community; comes in already primed to browse casually, not to solve an urgent problem.

**2. Emotional State:** Relaxed, curious, browsing for pleasure — this is leisure time, not task time.

**3. Behavior Pattern:** Scrolls and lingers. Reads descriptions, checks condition notes, may favorite several books before acting on one. Unlike a task-driven user, she doesn't want the browse experience minimized.

**4. Decision Criteria:** Condition trustworthiness (photos, honest description), a listing that signals the other person also cares about books (not just dumping stock), and the "will I actually get this" confidence.

**5. Success Outcome:** Finds something unexpected, trusts the condition description enough to commit, and the trade completes without incident — reinforcing that this is a *good* place to keep coming back to browse.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Goal 1 (Liquid Marketplace):** Highest browsing engagement of any persona — repeat browsing drives session frequency and keeps the "open to any book" pool active.
- ✅ **Goal 2 (Trust & Retention):** Her repeat-use potential is the highest of the three personas *if* early trades go well — she has no urgent deadline forcing a first try, so a bad first experience simply loses her instead of forcing a workaround.
- 🟡 **Goal 3 (Friction):** Lower stakes for her than for Tom or Priya — she's not trying to minimize time spent, she's trying to maximize discovery quality.

## Driving Forces

### Wants

**1. Discover unexpected books outside her usual habits**
*When browsing "open to any book" listings casually.*
**bookswap Promise:** Surface serendipitous matches and "someone like you also swapped" signals, not just search-driven results.
**Score:** 12/15 — MEDIUM

**2. Free up shelf space without feeling she's giving up on having read a book**
*Right after finishing a book, deciding whether to keep it.*
**bookswap Promise:** Frame trading as "passing it on," not "getting rid of it" — the book's story continues instead of ending.
**Score:** 9/15 — LOW

**3. Feel like a savvy reader who finds gems instead of paying retail**
*When mentally comparing to buying new.*
**bookswap Promise:** Show visible savings/value ("you saved $X vs. buying new") to reinforce the resourcefulness identity.
**Score:** 11/15 — MEDIUM

### Fears

**1. Receiving a book in worse condition than described**
*The moment she opens a swapped package.*
**bookswap Answer:** Structured condition ratings with required photos at listing time, plus a lightweight dispute/report flow.
**Score:** 13/15 — MEDIUM (highest of her six forces)

**2. A swap partner ghosting after she's already shipped her own book**
*While waiting on the other side of the trade.*
**bookswap Answer:** Delivery confirmation and reputation/history visible before committing to a trade partner.
**Score:** 11/15 — MEDIUM

**3. The platform feeling scammy or messy, tainting her "well-read" self-image**
*On first impression, before her first listing.*
**bookswap Answer:** Clean, trustworthy first-visit experience — reviews/ratings visible, no dark patterns, no clutter.
**Score:** 9/15 — LOW
