# Tom the Declutterer — Priority 3

> Target Group 3 of 3 · [← Back to Trigger Map](../trigger-map.md)

---

## Who Tom Is

Tom has a pile — or a shelf, or a box in the garage — of books he's not going to read again. Some he never finished. Some were gifts. He's not a book person the way Maya is; he just wants the physical clutter gone, and throwing books in the trash feels wasteful and vaguely guilt-inducing. He'd rather someone else get value from them, but he's not going to put real effort into making that happen.

## Psychological Profile

Tom is driven by **effort-avoidance and guilt-relief**, not book-love. Unlike Maya and Priya, he's often not seeking a book in return at all — he just wants the pile gone, ideally for a small reward (cash, a book he might read, or just the satisfaction of "handled"). His tolerance for friction is the lowest of all three personas: if listing feels like work — writing descriptions, taking multiple photos, negotiating — he will abandon and just donate the pile to a thrift store instead, which asks nothing of him.

He's also the most exposed to the practical realities of exchange: unlike buying online from a store, he has to actually hand over a physical book to a stranger, which raises questions he doesn't have good answers for (where do they meet, do I ship it, do I give out my address).

## Internal State

Before listing, Tom feels **mildly annoyed and vaguely guilty** — the books are just sitting there, and doing nothing about them nags at him. If listing is fast, he feels **satisfied and efficient** — task handled, box gone. If listing turns out to be effortful, he feels **irritated and disengaged** almost immediately, and quits without listing anything. At the point of arranging handoff/shipping, if address-sharing comes up, he feels **wary and exposed** — a stranger having his home address feels disproportionate to the value of a used paperback.

## Usage Context

**1. Access/Discovery:** Comes to bookswap specifically because he has books to get rid of — likely searched "sell/trade used books" or was referred by someone who used it to declutter.

**2. Emotional State:** Low patience, task-oriented, wants this *done*, not explored.

**3. Behavior Pattern:** Wants to list several books in one sitting (batch), minimal typing, minimal decisions per book.

**4. Decision Criteria:** How much effort does this take, and is my personal information protected? Price/reward is secondary to effort and safety.

**5. Success Outcome:** Lists a pile of books in a few minutes total, feels no anxiety about privacy, and the books get claimed without him having to manage a back-and-forth.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Goal 1 (Liquid Marketplace):** He *is* the supply side — without decluttterers like Tom listing books, there's no inventory for Maya to browse or Priya to search. Objective 1.1 (2,000 listings) depends almost entirely on making his flow effortless.
- 🟡 **Goal 2 (Trust & Retention):** Lower repeat-listing motivation than Maya (he may declutter once and be done) — but the address-privacy fear directly threatens Objective 2.2 (dispute rate) if mishandled.
- ✅ **Goal 3 (Friction):** The sharpest test case for every friction metric — Objective 3.1 (median time-to-list under 2 minutes) is essentially *his* success metric.

## Driving Forces

### Wants

**1. Get rid of books fast with almost no effort**
*Batch-listing a pile at once.*
**bookswap Promise:** A listing flow built for speed — bulk upload, minimal required fields, smart defaults (e.g., auto-filled title/author from ISBN scan).
**Score:** 14/15 — HIGH

**2. Feel his books go to someone who'll actually read them**
*Deciding list-vs-trash.*
**bookswap Promise:** Light framing ("3 people are looking for this book") that makes listing feel meaningfully better than the trash, without requiring extra effort from him.
**Score:** 9/15 — LOW

**3. Get some value back for minimal work**
*Weighing whether listing is even worth it.*
**bookswap Promise:** Make any return (cash, trade, or credit) frictionless to claim — the reward shouldn't require more effort than the listing did.
**Score:** 9/15 — LOW

### Fears

**1. The listing effort outweighing the payoff, causing abandonment mid-flow**
*During photos, description, condition, pricing steps.*
**bookswap Answer:** Radically minimize required steps; every additional field is a point of drop-off for this persona specifically. This is the single highest-scored force across all personas.
**Score:** 15/15 — HIGH (highest force overall)

**2. Sharing his home address with a stranger for pickup/shipping**
*At the point of arranging handoff/shipping.*
**bookswap Answer:** Address-masking, platform-mediated shipping labels, or locker/drop-off options so he never hands his address directly to a stranger. This is a documented real concern in book-exchange communities, not a hypothetical.
**Score:** 13/15 — MEDIUM (close to HIGH)

**3. Getting stuck negotiating with or waiting on a flaky counterpart**
*After listing, waiting for someone to claim it.*
**bookswap Answer:** Auto-claim/first-come flows with a time-boxed commitment window, reducing back-and-forth negotiation.
**Score:** 9/15 — LOW
