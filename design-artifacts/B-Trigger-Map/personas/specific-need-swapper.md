# Priya the Deadline Swapper — Priority 2

> Target Group 2 of 3 · [← Back to Trigger Map](../trigger-map.md)

---

## Who Priya Is

Priya needs *one specific book* — this semester's assigned textbook, next month's book-club pick, the third book in a trilogy she's mid-way through. She doesn't come to browse; she comes with a title already in mind and a clock running. She's price-sensitive (student budget, or just doesn't want to pay full price for something she'll read once) and has little patience for friction between "I need this" and "I have this."

## Psychological Profile

Priya's relationship to bookswap is **transactional and goal-directed**, the opposite of Maya's leisurely browsing. She's not here to discover — she's here to solve a specific, time-boxed problem. Her tolerance for uncertainty is low: if she can't tell quickly whether a match is likely, she'll abandon and go buy new or rent instead, even at higher cost, because **certainty is worth more to her than savings** when a deadline is close.

She's also pragmatic about condition — a textbook with highlighting is fine if the price is right and it arrives in time; she's not chasing a pristine copy the way Maya might be chasing a "gem."

## Internal State

When searching for her specific title, Priya feels **urgent and slightly anxious** — every day without a match is a day closer to needing a fallback plan. If she finds a match quickly, that anxiety flips to **relief and efficiency** — she got what she needed with minimal effort. If she doesn't, the anxiety turns to **frustration and impatience**, and she disengages fast.

## Usage Context

**1. Access/Discovery:** Arrives via direct search (Google, a classmate's recommendation, a book-club group) already knowing the exact title she needs.

**2. Emotional State:** Time-pressured, task-focused — this is an errand, not entertainment.

**3. Behavior Pattern:** Searches the specific title immediately, skips browsing entirely, wants a fast confirm/deny on availability.

**4. Decision Criteria:** Speed of match confirmation, price vs. buying new, and — if no exact match exists — whether there's a credible path to getting notified when one appears.

**5. Success Outcome:** Finds the exact title, confirms the trade/purchase, and receives it before her deadline.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Goal 1 (Liquid Marketplace):** Her use case is the sharpest test of match rate (Objective 1.2) — if the specific-title match rate is low, she churns immediately and doesn't come back.
- 🟡 **Goal 2 (Trust & Retention):** Lower repeat-use likelihood than Maya (her need is often one-off, e.g. a single semester's textbook) — but a good experience under time pressure builds outsized trust/word-of-mouth precisely because the stakes felt high.
- ✅ **Goal 3 (Friction):** The clearest persona for testing "quick matching" — she has zero patience for browsing friction.

## Driving Forces

### Wants

**1. Find the exact title before a deadline**
*Searching under time pressure (semester start, book-club date).*
**bookswap Promise:** Instant, precise title/ISBN search with real-time availability, not a browse-and-hope experience.
**Score:** 13/15 — MEDIUM

**2. Get it cheaper than buying new or renting**
*Comparing prices before committing.*
**bookswap Promise:** Clear price/savings comparison against buying new, shown at the point of decision.
**Score:** 11/15 — MEDIUM

**3. Get a fast yes/no on whether a match even exists**
*On her very first search for that title.*
**bookswap Promise:** Immediate search result — and if no match, an instant "notify me when available" option so she isn't left guessing.
**Score:** 14/15 — HIGH

### Fears

**1. Missing her deadline because no match materializes in time**
*As the deadline approaches with nothing found.*
**bookswap Answer:** Early, honest signal of match likelihood (e.g., how often this title turns up) so she can activate a backup plan in time instead of waiting blind.
**Score:** 12/15 — MEDIUM

**2. Paying into a swap that never ships**
*After agreeing to a trade and waiting.*
**bookswap Answer:** Delivery confirmation/tracking and a clear escalation path if a trade partner doesn't ship.
**Score:** 11/15 — MEDIUM

**3. Wasting time filtering irrelevant listings to find one specific title**
*On every visit to the site.*
**bookswap Answer:** Search-first UX for this persona — exact-title search prioritized over browse, so she never has to scroll past books she doesn't want.
**Score:** 13/15 — MEDIUM
